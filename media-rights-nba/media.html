The exponential growth journey of the NBA: Will the streaming focus of newest media rights keep viewers locked in, or trigger a profit plateau?

ByÂ Douglas Price>

July 15, 2024 - As reported by Tom Friend in Sports Business Journal (SBJ), the National Basketball Association (NBA) reached a historic 11-year, $76 billion agreement with ESPN, NBC Universal and Amazon to broadcast NBA and WNBA games. Barring a late change in circumstances, cable network TNT, subsidiary of Warner Brothers Discovery (WBD) will see its 40 year-plus run of NBA game telecasts come to an end. However, it is not likely to quietly fade into the background when the NBA Board of Governors meets tomorrow to ratify the deal.

The N.B.A. in-season tournament, now rebranded the Emirates NBA Cup, will be a feature of the new media rights deal for the 2025-26 season. This action was from last season's home debut for the Washington Wizards on November 17 at Capital One Arena. Credit: Douglas E. Price

Much of the lead up to this deal began last November with the inaugural in-season tournament, which has been renamed the Emirates NBA Cup for 2024 and beyond. The experiment drew significant increases in early season viewers of Tuesday and Friday night games. On the heels of the new media deal, the NBA Cup team groups and games were announced Thursday evening on ESPN. But the broadcast rights for that piece of the pie go to Amazon.



Room for new TV partners...and maybe a familiar face

The new deal will be a $1.8 billion investment for Amazon which is poised to make yet another push into streaming live sports beyond its deals for NFL regular season games on Thursday nights and holidays in the fall, and Major League Baseball from April through September. The other winners, ESPN and NBC/Peacock will pay $2.6 billion and $2.5 billion per year respectively.
